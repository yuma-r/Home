Twisted Nights

Details:
This is a point-and-click / sit-and-survive game made in Unity, inspired by the horror game series Five Nights at Freddy’s.

Game Idea:
You take on your first night shift in a restaurant where robots provide the entertainment. The goal is to survive the night without being killed by the robots. Designing a detailed layout of the building was particularly interesting. I learned how to bind UI elements to the position of objects in three-dimensional space.

Movement:
By clicking on markers, you move to that position and additional markers appear depending on the current position.

Game Mechanics:
Two camera systems each display a camera feed on a monitor. The cameras can be switched by clicking buttons on the displayed floor plan. Another mechanic is recharging and swapping batteries in a flashlight. One enemy can be driven away by the beam of light, while the other enemy kills you.

AI:
A global move timer determines the interval at which enemies attempt to move. When the timer reaches zero, a random number from 0 to 20 is chosen. If this number is less than or equal to the enemy’s difficulty level, it moves to the next point. The timer then resets.
